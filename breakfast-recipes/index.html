    Breakfast Recipes (ZOE)

    Summary: Six gut-friendly breakfast recipes from ZOE's Better Breakfast Guide, designed to provide fibre, protein, healthy fats, and plant diversity to start the day well.

    Sources: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf

    Last updated: 2026-04-15

    Overview

    These recipes are part of ZOE's Better Breakfast Guide. Each is designed to correct common breakfast mistakes — insufficient [[dietary-fibre]], too little protein, and high refined carbohydrates. Each recipe lists a plant count and, where shown, a ZOE App overall score.

    Recipe 1: Dark Cacao Chia Pudding

    By: Dr. Federica Amati, ZOE Head Nutritionist

    Why it's gut-friendly: Chia seeds are high in plant protein, all essential amino acids, omega-3 fatty acids, fibre, and [[polyphenols]]. Described as the "gut's favourite fat" — omega-3 fatty acids have a direct anti-inflammatory effect and the [[gut-microbiome]] is particularly fond of them.

    Plant count: 2+ (depending on toppings) | ZOE score: 100 (enjoy daily)

    Ingredients (serves 1): 2–3 tsp chia seeds, 1 tsp raw cacao powder, ½–¾ cup dairy milk / plant-based milk / kefir, optional frozen berries, optional honey.

    Method: Add chia seeds and cacao powder to a glass jar. Pour in milk or kefir. Add berries and honey if using. Refrigerate overnight. Eat the next day.

    Tips for seniors: Prepare the night before — no morning effort needed. Berries can be frozen (equally nutritious and easier to keep on hand).

    Recipe 2: Probiotic Bircher Muesli

    Why it's gut-friendly: Oats provide prebiotic fibre (see [[dietary-fibre]]). Kefir adds live cultures (see [[fermented-foods]]). Grated apple, mixed seeds, and optional raisins contribute plant diversity. 8g fibre per serving.

    Plant count: 5+ (depending on toppings) | ZOE score: 80 (enjoy regularly)

    Ingredients (serves 1–2): 1 apple (grated), 100g kefir, 2 tbsp Greek yoghurt, 1 tsp cinnamon, 50g oats, 50ml milk (or extra kefir), 40g mixed seeds, optional 25g raisins.

    Method: Grate apple into a large bowl. Mix in all remaining ingredients. Cover and refrigerate overnight (or at least a couple of hours). Add toppings to serve.

    Suggested toppings: Fresh or frozen fruit, nuts (almonds or walnuts), seeds (chia or flax), drizzle of honey or maple syrup.

    Recipe 3: Cottage Cheese Pancakes

    Why it's gut-friendly: Oats (complex carbohydrates) combined with cottage cheese (protein) slow sugar absorption and lead to more stable blood sugar. 27g protein per serving. Nut and berry toppings add healthy fats and fibre.

    Plant count: 6+ (depending on toppings) | Cooking time: 20 minutes

    Ingredients (serves 2): 50g oats, 1 banana (optional), 150g cottage cheese, 1 egg, pinch each of salt, baking powder, and cinnamon, splash of water or milk.

    Method: Blend oats to a fine flour. Add banana, cottage cheese, egg, salt, baking powder, cinnamon, and liquid. Blend until smooth. Cook in a non-stick pan with a little butter or oil until golden brown on each side. Top with Greek yoghurt, frozen raspberries, walnuts, figs, and a drizzle of honey.

    Recipe 4: Breakfast Hash

    Why it's gut-friendly: Black beans contain resistant starch that feeds gut microbes. Peppers contain flavonoids (a subclass of [[polyphenols]]) that support the gut lining. Anti-inflammatory spices (cumin, smoked paprika) add plant diversity.

    Plant count: 30+ | ZOE score: 90 (enjoy daily) | Cooking time: 20–30 minutes

    Ingredients (serves 2): 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, 1 small sweet potato (diced), 1 red pepper (chopped), ½ zucchini (diced), handful of cherry tomatoes, 1 can black beans (drained), ½ tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp cumin, ¼ cup crumbled feta, cilantro for garnish, optional 2 eggs.

    Method: Fry sweet potato in olive oil until cooked. Add pepper, zucchini, tomatoes, and spices; cook until soft. Stir in black beans for 2 minutes. Optional: crack eggs over the top and cover until whites are set. Top with feta and cilantro.

    Recipe 5: No Ordinary Avocado Toast

    Why it's gut-friendly: White beans add protein and resistant starch. Avocado provides healthy fats. Sourdough bread has a lower blood sugar impact than white bread. Pickled red onions or sauerkraut add [[fermented-foods]].

    Plant count: 30+ | Cooking time: 5–10 minutes

    Ingredients (serves 2): 1 can white beans (drained), 1 ripe avocado, juice and zest of 1 lemon, ½ tsp red pepper flakes, ½ tsp za'atar, 2 slices sourdough bread, salt and black pepper, pickled red onions or sauerkraut.

    Method: Mash white beans and avocado together with lemon juice, zest, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes. Spread on toasted sourdough. Top with sauerkraut or pickled red onions and za'atar.

    Recipe 6: Cinnamon Pecan Granola (Bonus)

    Why it's gut-friendly: Whole grains, nuts, and seeds provide fibre, healthy fats, and plant diversity. Cinnamon is an anti-inflammatory spice that contributes towards the 30-plants-a-week target.

    Tips for Seniors Over 75

    • Recipes 1 and 2 (chia pudding, Bircher muesli) can be prepared the evening before, requiring minimal morning effort.
    • Recipe 5 (avocado toast) takes under 10 minutes.
    • Frozen fruit and canned beans are convenient, equally nutritious alternatives to fresh.
    • All recipes can be adapted in portion size.
