    Gut-Friendly Recipes

    Summary: Five practical recipes from the ZOE Gut Health guide, each designed to incorporate multiple gut-health principles such as plant diversity, fibre, fermented foods, and polyphenols.

    Sources: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf

    Last updated: 2026-04-14

    Overview

    These recipes are provided by ZOE as practical examples of gut-friendly eating (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf). Each recipe combines multiple components of a healthy gut diet — [[plant-diversity]], [[dietary-fibre]], [[fermented-foods]], and [[polyphenols]].

    All recipes are simple, with a preparation time of 5–15 minutes.

    Recipe 1: Aubergine with Harissa Yoghurt and Roasted Chickpeas

    Prep time: 5 min | Total time: 35 min | Serves: 2

    Gut-health features: plant diversity (aubergine, tomatoes, garlic, parsley, spring onion, sesame seeds, pomegranate), fermented food (Greek yoghurt), polyphenols (olive oil, pomegranate)

    Ingredients: 1 aubergine, 1 tin chickpeas (400 g), 2 handfuls cherry tomatoes, 2 tsp harissa, 4 cloves garlic, 4 tbsp Greek yoghurt, 2 tbsp tahini, 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, 1 tbsp pomegranate seeds, 2 spring onions, 1 handful parsley, 1 tbsp sesame seeds, salt and pepper.

    Method: Toss aubergine, chickpeas, and tomatoes in harissa and olive oil; roast at 190°C for 30 min. Mix remaining harissa with yoghurt and roasted garlic. Mix tahini with water for a dressing. Serve aubergine and chickpeas over yoghurt, drizzled with tahini and topped with pomegranate, spring onion, parsley, and sesame seeds.

    Recipe 2: Creamy Courgette Butter Beans with Herby Tomato Salsa

    Prep time: 5 min | Total time: 25 min | Serves: 2

    Gut-health features: plant diversity (courgette, butter beans, tomatoes, dill, parsley, basil, chilli, pumpkin and sunflower seeds), fibre (butter beans), polyphenols (olive oil, tomatoes)

    Ingredients: 1 courgette, 2 shallots, 1 garlic clove, 2 tins butter beans (400 g each), 1 lemon, 10 cherry tomatoes, 1 tbsp capers, fresh dill, parsley and basil, 1 red chilli, 1 tbsp each pumpkin and sunflower seeds (toasted), extra virgin olive oil, salt and pepper.

    Method: Sauté shallots, add thinly sliced courgette, then garlic and butter beans with their liquid. Stir in lemon zest and simmer. Combine quartered tomatoes, capers, herbs, lemon, and olive oil for salsa. Spoon salsa over beans and top with toasted seeds and chilli.

    Recipe 3: Roasted Carrots with Tahini Yoghurt

    Prep time: 5 min | Total time: 35 min | Serves: 2

    Gut-health features: plant diversity (carrots, garlic, shallot, carrot tops), fermented food (Greek yoghurt), polyphenols (olive oil)

    Ingredients: 1 bunch carrots with tops, 1 head of garlic, 1 shallot, 2 tbsp tahini, 150 g Greek yoghurt, 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar, extra virgin olive oil, salt and pepper.

    Method: Roast carrots and garlic in olive oil at 180°C for 20–30 min. Finely chop shallot and carrot tops with apple cider vinegar and olive oil for a dressing. Mix tahini, yoghurt, and roasted garlic for the base. Serve carrots over tahini yoghurt with carrot top dressing.

    Recipe 4: Green Goddess Chickpea Sandwich

    Prep time: 10 min | Total time: 10 min | Serves: 1

    Gut-health features: plant diversity (avocado, parsley, dill, spring onions, chickpeas), fermented food (kefir), fibre (chickpeas)

    Ingredients: 1/4 avocado, 3 tbsp kefir, juice of 1/2 lemon, 1 garlic clove, handful each of parsley and dill, a splash of vinegar, 1 tin chickpeas (400 g), 2 spring onions, salt and pepper.

    Method: Blend avocado, kefir, lemon juice, garlic, vinegar, parsley, and dill until smooth. Pour dressing over chickpeas and mash together. Stir in additional herbs and spring onions. Use as a sandwich filling or salad topping.

    Recipe 5: Seedy Crackers with Beetroot Hummus

    Prep time: 15 min | Total time: 1 hr 15 min | Serves: 4

    Gut-health features: plant diversity (beetroot, chickpeas, flaxseed, chia seeds, mixed seeds), fibre (seeds, chickpeas), polyphenols (beetroot, lemon)

    Ingredients (crackers): 80 g ground flaxseed, 3 tbsp chia seeds, 100 g mixed seeds, 170 ml water, pinch of salt. Ingredients (hummus): 1 tin chickpeas (400 g), 1 beetroot (pre-cooked), 2 tbsp tahini, juice of 1 lemon, 1 garlic clove, a few ice cubes, salt.

    Method: Mix seeds with water, rest 15 min, roll thin between parchment, bake at 150°C for 1 hour. Cool before breaking into crackers. Blend all hummus ingredients until smooth. Serve crackers with beetroot hummus.
