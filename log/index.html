    2026-04-15 — Ingest: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf

    Source: ZOE "Better Breakfast Guide" — US edition (October 2025)

    Pages created: - better-breakfast-guide-zoe.md — source summary with key statistics, five breakfast swaps, recipe list, and expert advice - breakfast-recipes.md — concept page with full details of six breakfast recipes - blood-sugar.md — new concept page: blood sugar stability, breakfast composition, and relevance for seniors

    Pages updated: - dietary-fibre.md — added resistant starch section; added fibre-at-breakfast statistics; updated Sources and Related pages; updated Last updated date - index.md — added three new pages

    Key new concepts: - Resistant starch (black beans, cooled potatoes/rice, oats) — ferments in large intestine, feeding gut bacteria - Blood sugar stability as a breakfast-specific concern - Flavonoids (subclass of polyphenols) found in peppers

    Key statistics (from ZOE survey, source: 251030 Breakfast guide): - Only 6% of people get enough fibre at breakfast - Only 16% felt energetic after eating breakfast - 55% were hungry again within three hours

    Notes: This is the most practically useful source yet for seniors — overnight recipes (chia pudding, Bircher muesli) require minimal morning effort. High-protein breakfasts align with muscle-maintenance needs in adults over 75.
