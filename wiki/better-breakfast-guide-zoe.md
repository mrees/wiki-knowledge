# Better Breakfast Guide (Zoe, 2025)

**Summary**: ZOE's 2025 guide to gut-friendly breakfasts; six recipes, five science-approved swaps, statistics on common breakfast mistakes, and guidance on the importance of morning meal composition for blood sugar stability.

**Sources**: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-04-15

---

## Overview

This guide, published by ZOE in 2025, argues that most people start the day with a breakfast that is high in sugar and refined carbohydrates, low in protein and fibre, and missing opportunities for gut-supporting plant diversity. It offers practical fixes using simple swaps and easy-recipes.

## Key statistics

- **58%** of Americans start the day with too much sugar (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf).
- **80%** don't eat enough fibre at breakfast (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf).
- **70%** miss out on fermented foods (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf).
- **60%** don't get enough protein (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf).

## Why breakfast matters

Breakfast composition affects [[blood-sugar]] stability for the rest of the day. A high-sugar, low-protein breakfast produces a rapid blood sugar spike followed by a crash, leading to hanger and fatigue. A balanced breakfast with protein, fat, and [[dietary-fibre]] produces a gentler rise and a more steady energy curve.

## The four elements of a good breakfast

ZOE recommends that a gut-friendly breakfast includes (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf):

1. **Fibre** -- from oats, berries, seeds, or nuts; to feed beneficial gut bacteria.
2. **Protein** -- from eggs, yoghurt, cottage cheese, or legumes; to slow digestion and stabilise [[blood-sugar]].
3. **Healthy fats** -- from avocado, nuts, or olive oil; to support satiety and nutrient absorption.
4. **Plant diversity** -- an opportunity to add to the [[plant-diversity]] target of 30 plants a week.

## Five science-approved breakfast swaps

These are direct swaps recommended by ZOE's nutritionists (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf):

1. **Orange juice -> Whole fruit**: juice removes fibre and concentrates sugar; whole oranges provide fibre and slower sugar release.
2. **Sugary cereal -> Oats or muesli**: sugary cereals spike blood sugar; oats provide prebiotic fibre and sustained energy.
3. **White toast -> Sourdough or wholegrain**: white bread has a high glcyemic impact; sourdough and wholegrain bread digest more slowly and contain more fibre.
4. **Flavoured yoghurt -> Plain Greek yoghurt + fruit**: flavoured yoghurts are often high in added sugar; plain Greek yoghurt has more protein and live cultures (see [[fermented-foods]]).
5. **Processed meats -> Eggs or legumes**: bacon and sausages are high-risk [[ultra-processed-foods]]; eggs and legumes are high-protein whole foods.

## Key nutrient targets at breakfast

- **Protein**: Aim for at least 20g (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf).
- **Fibre**: Aim for 6–8g per breakfast (out of the 30g daily target) (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf).
- **Fermented foods**: Include at least one serving daily, e.g. kefir, live-yoghurt, or sauerkraut (see [[fermented-foods]]) (source: 251030 Breakfast 1761216573-zoe_better_breakfast_guide_us.pdf).

## Notes for seniors

This guide is especially relevant for adults over 75, as adequate protein intake at each meal supports muscle maintenance and prevents frailty. The swaps and recipes do not require complex cooking, and several can be prepared the night before (e.g. Birtcher muesli, chia pudding).
