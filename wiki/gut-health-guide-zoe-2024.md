# Gut Health Made Easy (ZOE, 2024)

**Summary**: A practical guide from ZOE outlining five evidence-informed dietary steps to improve gut health, with recipes, a shopping list, and a glossary of key terms.

**Sources**: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## Overview

This guide was produced by ZOE, a nutrition science company. It aims to make gut health accessible to a general audience by distilling research into simple, actionable steps. The guide states that good nutrition is about balance, not perfection.

## The Five Steps

The guide identifies five basic building blocks for a gut-friendly diet:

1. **Eat 30 different plants a week** — includes herbs, spices, fruits, vegetables, canned and frozen options, seeds, and nuts. See [[plant-diversity]].
2. **Try more colourful plants** — add a new colour to meals, include fruit in savoury dishes, cook with extra virgin olive oil. See [[polyphenols]].
3. **Increase your fibre intake** — make high-fibre swaps, use pulses and legumes, batch cook grains, keep skins on vegetables. See [[dietary-fibre]].
4. **Add more fermented foods** — small amounts daily, e.g. sauerkraut, kefir, yoghurt, fermented cheese. See [[fermented-foods]].
5. **Limit ultra-processed foods** — swap snacks, switch breakfast cereal for yoghurt and fruit, make homemade sauces. See [[ultra-processed-foods]].

## Shopping List

A gut-friendly shopping list covers: whole grains, beans and legumes, nuts and seeds, vegetables, fruit, herbs, spices, flavour boosters (garlic, ginger, onion, lemon), fermented foods (kefir, kimchi, miso, sauerkraut, yoghurt, kombucha), and other staples (extra virgin olive oil, apple cider vinegar, harissa).

## Glossary

The guide defines these terms (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf):

- **Digestive system**: organs responsible for breaking down food, absorbing nutrients, and eliminating waste.
- **Fermented food**: food changed by beneficial bacteria or yeasts that break down starches and sugars.
- **Fibre**: a carbohydrate in plant foods that the body cannot digest; supports gut microbiome and bowel regularity.
- **Gut health**: the well-being of the digestive system, including gut lining, microbiome composition, and ability to digest and absorb nutrients.
- **Gut microbiome**: the community of trillions of bacteria and other microbes in the digestive system.
- **Plant diversity**: the variety of plants eaten, including fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, herbs, and spices.
- **Polyphenols**: compounds in plants that provide colour, offer health benefits, and may promote beneficial gut bacteria.
- **Prebiotics**: types of fibre that feed beneficial bacteria; found in oats, garlic, onions, leeks, and asparagus.
- **Probiotic foods**: foods containing live microorganisms (bacteria or yeasts) that provide health benefits.
- **Ultra-processed foods (UPFs)**: manufactured products with many artificial ingredients and additives; typically high in sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats.
