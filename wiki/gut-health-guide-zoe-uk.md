# Gut Health Made Easy — UK Edition (ZOE, 2025)

**Summary**: The UK version of ZOE's "Gut Health Made Easy" guide. Content is nearly identical to the 2024 US edition but includes a more comprehensive gut-friendly shopping list with additional plant varieties.

**Sources**: 250820 uk-gut-health-guide.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-04-15

---

## Overview

This is the UK-market edition of ZOE's gut health guide. It covers the same five dietary steps, five recipes, and glossary as the US edition (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf). The primary difference is an expanded shopping list and UK conventions (Celsius temperatures, British spelling such as "courgette", "fibre").

For full content details, see [[gut-health-guide-zoe-2024]].

## Expanded Shopping List

The UK edition shopping list is more comprehensive than the US edition. Additional items not in the US list include (source: 250820 uk-gut-health-guide.pdf):

**Whole grains**: Amaranth, Freekeh, Pasta (wholemeal, brown rice, chickpea, lentil), Sorghum, Teff, Wild rice

**Beans and legumes**: Black-eyed peas, Broad beans, Haricot beans, Pinto beans, Red kidney beans

**Vegetables**: Avocado, Brussels sprouts, Celery, Courgette, Cucumber, Lettuce, Purple cabbage, Radish, Sweetcorn

**Fruit**: Grapes (green and red), Melon (cantaloupe, honeydew), Peach, Pear, Pineapple, Plum

**Nuts and seeds**: Cashews, Hemp seeds, Nut butters (peanut, almond, cashew), Pine nuts, Pistachios, Walnuts

**Fermented foods**: Pickles, Tempeh (in addition to the US list)

**Flavour boosters**: Capers, Jalapeño, Lime, Roasted peppers, Sun-dried tomatoes

**Herbs**: Chives, Oregano, Sage

**Spices**: Cayenne pepper, Chilli powder, Cloves, Nutmeg

This expanded list is useful for seniors aiming to reach 30 different plants per week, as it highlights many convenient options (canned, frozen, or shelf-stable).

## Recipes

The five recipes are identical to the US edition. See [[gut-friendly-recipes]] for details.

## Key difference from US edition

The US edition notes temperatures in Fahrenheit; the UK edition uses Celsius. Recipe ingredients and methods are otherwise the same.
