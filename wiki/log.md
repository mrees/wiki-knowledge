- `index.md` — added three new pages

**Key new concepts**:
- Resistant starch (black beans, cooled potatoes/rice, oats) — ferments in large intestine, feeding gut bacteria
- Blood sugar stability as a breakfast-specific concern
- Flavonoids (subclass of polyphenols) found in peppers

**Key statistics** (from ZOE survey, source: 251030 Breakfast guide):
- Only 6% of people get enough fibre at breakfast
- Only 16% felt energetic after eating breakfast
- 55% were hungry again within three hours

**Notes**: This is the most practically useful source yet for seniors — overnight recipes (chia pudding, Bircher muesli) require minimal morning effort. High-protein breakfasts align with muscle-maintenance needs in adults over 75.
