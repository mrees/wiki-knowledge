# Plant Diversity

**Summary**: The variety of plant foods eaten in the diet, including fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, herbs, and spices. Eating 30 different plants per week is a key recommendation for gut health.

**Sources**: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## What is plant diversity?

Plant diversity refers to the variety of plants eaten in the diet. This includes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, herbs, and spices (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf).

A diverse range of plant foods feeds a wider variety of beneficial gut bacteria, which supports a healthy [[gut-microbiome]].

## The 30-plants-a-week target

The ZOE guide recommends eating 30 different plants per week as a key step for gut health (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf). This does not mean 30 large servings — herbs, spices, seeds, and nuts all count.

Practical tips from the guide:
- Add different herbs and spices to meals
- Try a new fruit or vegetable every week
- Explore canned and frozen options (these count equally)
- Make a mix of seeds and nuts to add to meals

## Why variety matters

Different plants contain different types of [[dietary-fibre]] and [[polyphenols]]. Eating a wide range gives gut bacteria more diverse "fuel", which supports a richer and more resilient microbiome.

## Gut-friendly shopping list (plants)

The guide provides a comprehensive list. Key categories include (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf):

- **Whole grains**: oats, brown rice, quinoa, rye, spelt, farro, buckwheat, bulgur wheat, millet, barley, and others
- **Beans and legumes**: chickpeas, lentils, butter beans, black beans, cannellini beans, edamame, and others
- **Vegetables**: artichoke, asparagus, aubergine, beetroot, broccoli, carrots, cauliflower, kale, pumpkin, spinach, sweet potato, and others
- **Fruit**: apple, banana, blueberries, kiwi, mango, orange, pomegranate, raspberries, strawberries, and others
- **Herbs**: basil, coriander, dill, mint, parsley, rosemary, thyme, and others
- **Spices**: cinnamon, cumin, turmeric, cardamom, paprika, and others

## Practical tips for seniors

Canned and frozen vegetables and legumes count towards the 30 plants and require less preparation, making them a convenient option.
