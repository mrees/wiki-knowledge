## What are polyphenols?

Polyphenols are a group of compounds found in plants that provide colour, offer health benefits, and may promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf).

They are the reason colourful fruits and vegetables are particularly good for gut health. Different colours in plants typically signal different types of polyphenols, which is why eating a variety of colourful plants is recommended.

## Polyphenols and the gut microbiome

Polyphenols act as a food source for beneficial gut bacteria, supporting a healthy [[gut-microbiome]]. They also contribute to [[plant-diversity]] benefits beyond [[dietary-fibre]] alone.

## How to get more polyphenols

The ZOE guide recommends (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf):

- Add a new colour to one of your meals each day
- Include a portion of fruit in a savoury meal
- Buy a different coloured version of a familiar vegetable (e.g. purple carrots, red cabbage)
- Cook or dress food with **extra virgin olive oil**, which is also rich in polyphenols

## Good sources of polyphenols

From the ZOE shopping list and recipes (source: 241101 Gut-Health-Guide-29-12-23.pdf):

- Berries (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries)
- Pomegranate
- Red and purple vegetables (beetroot, red cabbage, aubergine, purple carrots)
- Dark leafy greens (kale, spinach)
- Extra virgin olive oil
- Herbs and spices (turmeric, cinnamon, oregano, rosemary)
- Dark chocolate and green tea (not mentioned in this guide but well established in literature — needs verification)
